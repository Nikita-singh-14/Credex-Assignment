# Unit Economics

## 1. Converted Lead Value (LTV)
- **Estimate**: $300 - $600 per startup.
- **Reasoning**: If the average startup using the Audit Tool has a standard AI spend of ~$500/mo, their Credex spend is ~$415/mo. Assuming Credex takes a 5% transaction/platform fee on managed billing, Credex earns ~$20/mo per startup. Assuming an average customer lifespan of 18-24 months before they graduate to enterprise contracts or churn, the LTV is roughly **$360 to $480**.

## 2. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) by Channel
- **Organic Twitter/X (Viral Loop)**: ~$0.00 CAC. Time cost only.
- **Cold Outbound (DMs)**: ~$5.00 CAC (cost of founder's time / VA outreach tools).
- **Paid Ads (LinkedIn/X)**: ~$45.00 - $70.00 CAC per qualified booked consultation.

## 3. Profitability Conversion Rates
To remain profitable via paid channels, the math requires:
- **Audit Completed**: 1,000 users
- **Lead Captured**: 30% (300 emails)
- **Consultation Booked**: 10% of leads (30 booked calls)
- **Credit Purchase / Conversion**: 30% of calls (9 closed won)
- **Total Revenue**: 9 startups * $400 LTV = $3,600. 
- *If paid traffic cost $2,000 to get 1,000 clicks ($2 CPC), we are profitable.*

## 4. Driving $1M ARR in 18 Months
To hit $1M ARR:
- Assuming an average ARPU (Annual Revenue Per User) of $240 (from the $20/mo cut).
- We need **4,166 active startups** successfully routed through Credex unified billing.
- With a 3% end-to-end conversion rate from landing page visitor to closed deal, we need ~138,000 unique visitors over 18 months, or roughly **7,600 visitors per month**.
- This is highly achievable if the Viral Loop (the dynamic OpenGraph image showing specific dollar savings) achieves a viral coefficient > 0.4.
